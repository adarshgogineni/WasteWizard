Frequently Asked Questions:

Lots of things are recyclable. The Environmental Protection Agency tracks the recycling of paper, paperboard, glass, metals, plastics, rubber, leather, textiles, wood, food, yard trimmings, and more. It’s hard to know what to do with what.
Did you know the recycling rate for plastic in the United States sits dismally low? According to estimates by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), less than 10 percent of consumed plastic is being recycled. Instead, the majority of plastic is either incinerated or relegated to landfills, as only a select few types of plastic can be recycled.
Ever wondered if that trusty three-arrow symbol on your product means it's automatically recyclable? Turns out, not necessarily. Some sneaky companies slap that symbol on non-recyclable goods, leaving consumers in the dark and recycling systems overloaded. In fact, California recently took legislative action to combat this false advertising. Still wondering if your plastic can be recycled? Fret not! With Waste Wizard, you can swiftly figure out what's recyclable and what's not, empowering you to make informed choices and ease the burden on recycling facilities.
Waste Wizard is recycling guide web-application that leverages image recognition technology for material identification, enabling users to scan different recycling symbols with their phones' cameras. With the integration of a pre-trained machine learning API, the app employs a dataset of recycling symbols to deliver information on how the scanned item can be recycled, if at all. Waste Wizard both empowers users in making informed waste disposal decisions and instills confidence in their environmental practices.
Recycling isn’t a perfect system, but there are other ways to combat plastic pollution. The EPA's Draft National Strategy to Prevent Plastic Pollution. emphasizes reducing, reusing, collecting, and capturing plastic waste. Explore options such as choosing products with minimal packaging, opting for reusable alternatives, and supporting businesses that prioritize sustainable practices. Additionally, advocate for policies that promote plastic reduction and participate in community clean-up efforts to prevent plastic from entering waterways. By actively embracing these strategies and using WasteWizard, you play a crucial role in mitigating plastic pollution and fostering a more sustainable future.
